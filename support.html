How you can help⤵

It may not have reached the public consciousness yet, but those in the industry see that we've entered a new age; the Creative Revolution is in full swing. The corporate structure and capabilities born of the previous Industrial Revolution are no longer restricted to those with the money and social influence to grasp them. The availability of the Internet, DIY culture and the advent of crowd-funding have all converged to provide unprecedented opportunities for the average person.

Years ago I came up with an idea I call distributed micro-income. Today I am embarking on that Grand Experiment. My goal is to establish a diverse collection of income streams that together total approximately what I currently make in working at a single, full-time job. This page represents one aspect of the overall plan: Leveraging crowd-funding to work for you - all of you. The world at large. In the same way that people give to pay for schools, clean sidewalks, feed the hungry and all the other things that benefit society as a whole, I'm asking you to help support me so I can:

  • Create, maintain and improve open source software.
  • Write educational tutorials, books and documentation.
  • Make instructional, informative videos.
  • Produce entertaining novels, short stories, videos, etc.

And so that all of these things can be made available to everyone - free of charge. This is not an exhaustive list. I hope to come up with new ideas and discover additional talents, but this represents a good starting point.

I don't expect to be able to do all of this solely with donated funds. I plan to do some freelance work-for-hire, commissioned work and maybe even a part-time job. The idea, remember, is to have a distributed base of income. This page, however, specifically the crowd-sourcing aspect of the overall plan. It also represents my first steps down this new path as I expect it to take awhile to build to the point where it produces enough income to be relevant.

Hopefully, you will join me.

How you can help

I estimate that I need approximately $100k/year overall (from all income sources) to replace what I currently get from traditional employment - including expenses like healthcare insurance, tax payments, etc. This roughly breaks down to around $8k/month or $2k/week. My plan is for a portion of this income to come from crowd-funding sources like the ones suggested here.

What you can expect

I won't lie to you; this is all going to build slowly - for me as well as you. It's a Catch-22, chicken-and-egg situation: I need to raise enough money to be able to quit my job, so I have time to spend on all the work you're funding me to do. But, of course, you'd like to see some results before you dump a bunch of money into my pocket. That's reasonable.

Hopefully my current resume and existing work history, even if established through more traditional means, gives you some feel for my skill and abilities. I'm also working on a few ideas to provide as much transparency as possible and maybe even offer some incentives for higher contribution levels. It's still early stages, but here are some of the ideas I've been thinking about:

  • Regular, public stand-ups - in the style of Agile Scrum. A friend suggested IRC, but I'm also considering an audio recording; kind of a mini-podcast.
  • A donation level that allows voting on what I will work on. This (and the next idea) would likely be done through Patreon.
  • A donation level that can request projects/bugs/features for me to work on.
I'm also open to hearing your ideas. While I do expect to also be doing freelance work-for-hire, I'd like to keep these funding efforts in the realm of Open Source so everybody can benefit.

And, of course, you're always free to withdraw your support. Both of the patron-based services I've chosen allow you to cancel your recurring payment at any time. I hope that as we travel down this road together I don't give you reason to do so, but I also understand that it's your money and you can do whatever you want with it. With any luck we can use it make the world a better place :-)
